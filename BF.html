Performers

Retromantics

Craig Dickinson - Saxophone

Craig is an instrumentalist and vocalist originally hailing from Sydney, Australia. Craig has been performing professionally since he was a young lad, combining soulful vocals and funky saxophone for various bands throughout the years.

Teddy Florendo - Drums

Teddy Florendo has been performing as a drummer and percussionist for over 20 years. He started back in the Philippines at the tender age 14 years playing with various artists and bands in the rock and pop genre. Arriving in the UAE in 2017 he got involved in the jazz music scene right away and to this day continues to work with many different musicians and artists in a wide variety of musical styles.

Artie Poghosyan - Keyboard

Artie started learning the Piano at the age of 7 in Armenia.
Originally classically trained, Artie is an accomplished jazz musician and composer in various musical styles, having studied and performed in London, UK.
Since moving to Abu Dhabi in 2009, Artie has been busy playing across the country, with the Abu Dhabi Big Band, The Swing Revue, Cool's The Rule, Jazzy Solea and dozens of other bands.
As well as playing the Piano, Artie is a multi-instrumentalist and also plays the Bass, Guitar and Drums.
Artie holds a Fellowship award from London College of Music and Licentiate award from Trinity College London.

Kalana De Silva

Kalana has been teaching guitar since 2009 and is currently Head of Guitar at The British School Al Khubairat.
Kalana has performed with various ensembles and groups over the years. He is proficient in both Classical and Bass Guitar which enables him to adapt to any genre of music including Classical, Jazz Pop and Orchestral.
Kalana is known throughout the region for his passionate approach to all things musical - he has been teaching in Abu Dhabi for the past 22 years and has taught several international competition winners. Currently he performs with Abu Dhabi Big Band, Little Band, Saadiyat Big Band and also works with small ensemble groups such as The Opera Trio, Ana and The Strings and Take 3. He had the pleasure of performing with the renowned artist Briana Corrigan (of The Beautiful South) in The Collective.

Blue Fever

Blue Fever is an Abu Dhabi-based a cappella ensemble formed originally in 2008.

The group is made up of very dedicated and experienced singers passionate about creating the finest 4 to 8 part harmonies.

They primarily focus on jazz and popular music

Louise Creedon - Soprano

Louise hails from Cork, Ireland; land of the wee leprechaun and tea…the latter of which she loves, but only if it's Barry's tea and with low fat milk. She has been singing since she was old enough to drink tea. Louise is also a first grade teacher and is quite happy that those humans are tinier than her. When she is not singing or teaching tiny humans, you can find her drinking tea, reading and chilling by a pool (separately or simultaneously). She loves to sing most kinds of music although Metallica is somewhat out of her wheelhouse. This is her fourth year in Blue Fever and the tea break is her favourite part of rehearsals.

Did we mention she loves tea?

Helen Kerr - Soprano

In addition to being the Director of Blue Fever, she is the Director of Music at the British School Al Khubairat and Directs the Abu Dhabi Junior and Senior Youth Choirs; she plays the saxophone, clarinet and orchestral percussion; has a background in classical singing; and in her spare time is involved in big bands, ensembles and choirs. She is passionate about directing choirs, including the Special Olympics and Expo closing ceremonies, and is taking over as Director of the choral society, The Al Khubairat Singers. She starred as the Witch in Hansel and Gretel in Abu Dhabi's National Theatre and has performed as a soloist in Muscat, the Royal Albert Hall, The Sage Gateshead and other prestigious UK venues. Helen also finds time to be a wife and mother of two, and loves swimming, cooking and being outdoors

Josefin Persson - Soprano

Josefin has been with Blue since 2021. She started performing at only 4 years old when her parents enrolled her in her first singing group! From then on, she has been passionate singer both within groups and as a soloist. In her home country (Sweden) she has worked as a backing vocalist for several famous Swedish artists and performed on television. She has also recorded music and performed her own concerts wherever life’s great adventure has brought her. Aside from music, Josefin also enjoys her work as an Events Project Manager and is has a huge love of animals, dancing, food and wine. Her quest for adventure is never distant and her favourite hobbies include travelling, horseback riding and some occasional paragliding!

Nicholas Wyon - Tenor

Nicholas is an exceptionally busy ICU doc who enjoys removing his N95 and subjecting any captive audience to high- pitched keening noise (having been a “keen” tenor for the best part of his adult years).
Apparently, this is a highly effective, if lesser-known, traditional Swedish technique for catching fish…
During his (limited) spare time, he spends his days out on the water.

Manoj Singanayagam< - Tenor

Manoj joined the Blue Fever family last year. He hails from island paradise of Sri Lanka, but has called UAE home for the last decade and a half. By day, he is crunching numbers at the bank, by night, he swaps the spreadsheets for music scores. When the numbers start to blur, he turns up the volume and let the melodies balance his sanity. From choral groups to church choirs, he's sung his heart out across every genre of music. He is also a keen thespian and was part of several amateur theatre groups back in Sri Lanka. Besides music, his other forms of escape are netflix, sports and travel.

Brent Walker - Tenor

Brent has been singing with ensembles since he was a kid growing up in the late 70s in the suburbs of LA.
He has lived in Abu Dhabi since 2015 and is lucky to be in his third season with Blue Fever.
Sometimes a tenor, sometimes a baritone, Brent enjoys the challenge of finding that moment when voices blend to create something special.

Sally O'Neill - Alto

Sally joined Blue Fever in 2010. A Chartered Surveyor by trade, Sally adores nothing more than to leave the day job in construction behind to partake of her favourite hobby - caterwauling! She has been with BF since 2010, having arrived in Abu Dhabi FIFTEEN or so years ago directly from her homeland of Scotland where, ever since her schooldays, she has only ever sung in the shower.
She also loves her rescue mutts, holidays in far flung locations, boating, reading, skiing, parlour games and costume parties.

Laura Roberts - Alto

The “Big Momma” of the group, Laura Roberts is passionate about the arts and has both actively promoted and produced many musical events in Abu Dhabi over the last 25 years. As a keen advocate for community-based arts she formed ArtsNetUAE, which strives to connect and support visual and performance artists. She and friends founded Blue Fever in 2008 having previously formed another a cappella group, “Voices of Harmony” an all-women's barbershop group, in 2005.
Laura feels that the support and love from our ensemble through these trying and scary times kept us positive and full of hope and she can't thank them enough!!!

Lisa Rae Scheirer - Alto

Lisa Rae Scheirer - Lisa Rae is a proud Canadian from Vancouver BC (the most beautiful place on earth). She has been in the UAE for quite a few years and has lived in Dubai, Ras Al Khaima and now Abu Dhabi. Singing is in Lisa Rae's blood. Not only did she inherit her father's rich, deep voice, she has also been singing solos and singing harmony in groups for as long as she can remember. Lisa Rae loves to be involved in music wherever she is. She sang with the Dubai Chamber Choir, with whom she made a trip to NY and sang in Carnegie Hall; she has also joined the Abu Dhabi Choral Group where she will try out her acting and singing chops as Mother Superior in ADCG’s production of Sister Act - A Divine Musical this June 6, 7, 8. Lisa Rae feels priviledged to have been singing with Blue Fever this past year and she is looking forward to many more years to come.

Steve Haynes - Bass

A Proud English/Welsh/Irish man, Steve has been making music for nearly 50 years! (He alleges he started when he was very young). Originally a guitarist, he branched out into bass, banjo, mandolin, ukulele and fiddle; basically, anything with strings. Steve has played in a wide variety of different bands over the years, covering numerous musical genres ranging from Welsh folk to hard rock. He is enjoying broadening his musical horizons singing and playing with Blue Fever and away from music, strings come to the fore (oops, wrong sport) once more as Steve is very keen tennis player.

David Nunn - Bass

David is a British/Irish music-loving lawyer who has been in legal practice in Abu Dhabi for more than 18 years. He enjoys music of all genres, styles and from all time - periods, and is always up for some Karaoke!
David is the most recent addition to the group but previously sang and acted in Musicals with the Abu Dhabi Choral Group (having temporarily retired to spend more time with his young children - who are now also getting into Musical Theatre courtesy of the great team at BSAK and singing musical numbers in the car with their dad!). David finds singing with Blue Fever to be great 'stress therapy' and the ideal relief from his intense day job.
